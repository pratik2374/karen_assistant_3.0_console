# Summary of Findings
To prepare for placements at top tech companies, B.Tech students should focus on Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA), system design basics, resume building, personal projects, and mock interviews. Utilize online resources like LeetCode, Pramp, and GeeksforGeeks for DSA and system design practice. Build a strong resume by highlighting projects and skills. Create personal projects on GitHub to demonstrate coding abilities.

---
# Detailed Report
## Step 1: DSA and Interview Prep
- **LeetCode**: A popular platform for practicing DSA with a large collection of problems and a discussion forum for learning from others.
- **Pramp**: Offers free mock interviews and coding challenges to improve interview skills.
- **GeeksforGeeks**: Provides a wide range of practice problems, interview experiences, and study materials for DSA and other computer science topics.

## Step 2: System Design Basics
- **System Design Primer**: A free online book that covers the fundamentals of system design, including scalability, availability, and maintainability.
- **Groking the System Design Interview**: A comprehensive resource that includes topics like database design, microservices architecture, and more.

## Step 3: Resume Tips
- **Resume.io**: Offers resume building tips, examples, and templates tailored for tech jobs.
- **Glassdoor**: Provides insights into what recruiters look for in a resume, along with examples of successful resumes.

## Step 4: Project Ideas
- **GitHub**: Showcase personal projects here. Project ideas can range from simple command-line tools to complex web applications.
- **First Timers Only**: A website listing open-source projects that are perfect for beginners, helping to build a portfolio.

## Step 5: Mock Interview Resources
- **InterviewBit**: Provides mock interviews, problems to practice, and a community to discuss interview experiences.
- **HackerRank**: Offers coding challenges and mock interviews in various programming languages and domains.

## Actionable 2-4 Week Plan
1. **Week 1**: Focus on DSA. Solve 10-15 problems on LeetCode daily. Start reading system design basics.
2. **Week 2**: Continue DSA practice. Dedicate 2 days to system design study and start working on personal projects.
3. **Week 3**: Practice whiteboarding exercises for system design. Finalize and start applying for internships or jobs.
4. **Week 4**: Mock interviews and final preparations. Utilize Pramp and InterviewBit for mock interviews.

Remember, consistent practice and a well-structured approach are key to success in placement preparations. Utilize the mentioned resources effectively, and always keep learning and improving. 

### Summary
To get placed at a top tech company, a B.Tech student should focus on DSA/interview prep, system design basics, resume tips, project ideas, and mock interview resources. Utilize high-quality resources such as LeetCode, GeeksforGeeks, and SystemDesignPrimer. Prioritize free or high-signal resources like MIT OpenCourseWare and Stanford University's CS229 course.

*   Tailor your resume to the specific job description, highlighting relevant skills and experiences.
*   Use a clean and concise format, with bullet points and white space to improve readability.
*   Include relevant projects, internships, or coding challenges to demonstrate skills.

#### Project Ideas
*   Build a personal website or blog to showcase skills and experiences.
*   Contribute to open-source projects on GitHub or Bitbucket to demonstrate collaboration and coding skills.
*   Develop a mobile app or game to demonstrate skills in a specific area.

*   Pramp: Offers free mock interviews and coding challenges.
*   Glassdoor: Provides interview questions, reviews, and salary information for various tech companies.
